1. What a Sallen-Key Stage Looks Like (Reference)

Each stage of the 4th-order Butterworth uses a unity-gain Sallen-Key low-pass topology. The key is that C_feedback connects from the op-amp output back to the R-R junction node, not to ground.

3. Diagnosis Summary

✓ What's Correct The R-R series chain topology is right for both stages. R3→R4 in series with C13 teeing off the junction, and R5→R6 in series with C15 teeing off the junction — that IS the correct Sallen-Key passive arrangement. The component values are also correct (1.62k/5.76k/11n/1.1n for Stage 1, 17.4k/536Ω/11n/1.1n for Stage 2). PGA bypass caps (C11→AVDD, C12→DVDD, both to GND) are also correct.
⚠ Critical Error: C14 Pin2 is wired to GND C14 is at position (125.73, 76.20) with rotation 270°. Its Pin 2 lands at (125.73, 88.90) where a GND power symbol sits. In a Sallen-Key filter, C14 is the feedback capacitor — it connects from Node B (the R4 output / op-amp +IN junction) back to the op-amp output (OUT_A, Pin 1). Grounding the feedback cap turns this into a passive 2-pole RC filter with no Q control. The filter's frequency response will be wrong — it won't achieve the Butterworth shape (Q=1.306) and the attenuation at 50 kHz will be much less than the designed −41.8 dB.
⚠ Floating Pins: C13.Pin2, C15.Pin2, C16.Pin2 The shunt caps (C13, C15) each have one pin correctly at the R-R junction, but the other pin is floating — it needs to connect to GND. The feedback cap C16 has both pins floating on the GND side (Pin2 at 119.38, 104.14 goes nowhere). C16 should connect from Node B' to OUT_B.
⚠ No connections to LTC6242 yet All 16 pins of U_FILT are unconnected. The passive filter networks are grouped to the left of the IC but no wires reach the op-amp pins. This includes: +IN_A, −IN_A, OUT_A (Stage 1), +IN_B, −IN_B, OUT_B (Stage 2), V+, V−, and the unused section tying (Pins 10↔11, 15↔16, 12→GND, 14→GND).
